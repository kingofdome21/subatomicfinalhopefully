MUON

μ-

Mass: 105.7 MeV/c²

Charge: -1 e

  1. The muon is a second-generation lepton, similar to the electron but much heavier.
  2. It has a negative electric charge.
  3. Muons are unstable and decay quickly into an electron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino.
  4. They are produced in high-energy collisions, such as cosmic rays hitting the atmosphere.

TAU

τ-

Mass: 1776.8 MeV/c²

Charge: -1 e

  1. The tau is a third-generation lepton, even heavier than the muon.
  2. It has a negative electric charge.
  3. Tau particles are very unstable and decay rapidly into other particles.
  4. They were discovered in the 1970s.

ELECTRON NEUTRINO

νe

Mass: Very small (exact value unknown)

Charge: 0 e

  1. The electron neutrino is a first-generation lepton with no electric charge.
  2. It interacts very weakly with matter, making it hard to detect.
  3. Neutrinos are produced in nuclear reactions, like those in the sun.
  4. They have a tiny mass, but it is not zero.

MUON NEUTRINO

νμ

Mass: Very small (exact value unknown)

Charge: 0 e

  1. The muon neutrino is a second-generation lepton associated with the muon.
  2. It has no electric charge and interacts weakly.
  3. Muon neutrinos are produced in processes involving muons.

TAU NEUTRINO

ντ

Mass: Very small (exact value unknown)

Charge: 0 e

  1. The tau neutrino is a third-generation lepton associated with the tau particle.
  2. It has no electric charge and interacts weakly.
  3. Tau neutrinos are the most recently discovered type of neutrino.

PHOTON

γ

Mass: 0

Charge: 0 e

  1. The photon is the force carrier for the electromagnetic force.
  2. It is a massless particle with no electric charge.
  3. Light is composed of photons.
  4. Photons travel at the speed of light in a vacuum.

GLUON

g

Mass: 0

Charge: 0 e

  1. Gluons are the force carriers for the strong nuclear force.
  2. They bind quarks together within protons and neutrons.
  3. Gluons are massless and have no electric charge, but they carry "color charge".
  4. There are eight types of gluons.

W BOSON

W±

Mass: 80.4 GeV/c²

Charge: ±1 e

  1. W bosons (W+ and W-) are force carriers for the weak nuclear force.
  2. They are responsible for certain types of radioactive decay.
  3. W bosons are very heavy and have either a positive or negative electric charge.
  4. They were discovered in 1983.

Z BOSON

Z0

Mass: 91.2 GeV/c²

Charge: 0 e

  1. The Z boson is also a force carrier for the weak nuclear force.
  2. It is involved in processes like neutrino scattering.
  3. The Z boson is very heavy and has no electric charge.
  4. It was discovered along with the W bosons in 1983.

HIGGS BOSON

H0

Mass: 125.1 GeV/c²

Charge: 0 e

  1. The Higgs boson is associated with the Higgs field, which gives mass to other fundamental particles.
  2. It has no electric charge and is very heavy.
  3. The Higgs boson was discovered at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) in 2012.
  4. Its discovery confirmed the final piece of the Standard Model of particle physics.
